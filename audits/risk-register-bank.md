# Risk Register Assessment – Commercial Bank

## Project Description

In this exercise, I performed a risk assessment for a commercial bank by evaluating potential threats to a critical asset: the bank’s funds. Using a structured risk register, I assessed the likelihood and severity of each risk and calculated a priority score to determine which risks require the most immediate attention.

This activity demonstrates an understanding of risk analysis, prioritisation, and applying the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) approach to identifying and managing security risks.

---

## Operational Environment

The bank operates in a coastal area with low crime rates but faces environmental risks such as natural disasters. It has 100 on-premise employees and 20 remote employees, increasing exposure to both internal and external threats. The bank manages sensitive financial data for over 2,000 individual and 200 commercial accounts and must comply with strict financial regulations.

---

## Risk Register

| Asset | Risk | Description | Likelihood | Severity | Priority |
|------|------|------------|------------|----------|----------|
| Funds | Business email compromise | An employee is tricked into sharing confidential information | 2 | 3 | 6 |
| Funds | Compromised user database | Customer data is poorly encrypted | 3 | 3 | 9 |
| Funds | Financial records leak | A backup database server is publicly accessible | 3 | 3 | 9 |
| Funds | Theft | The bank's safe is left unlocked | 2 | 2 | 4 |
| Funds | Supply chain disruption | Delivery delays due to natural disasters | 1 | 2 | 3 |

---

## Risk Analysis

Security events are possible due to the bank’s reliance on multiple employees and systems to handle sensitive financial data, increasing the risk of human error and social engineering attacks such as business email compromise. Additionally, the bank stores large volumes of customer and financial data, making it a valuable target for attackers. Environmental risks such as natural disasters can also impact operations, particularly due to the bank’s coastal location.

---

## Key Findings

- **Highest priority risks (9):**
  - Compromised user database  
  - Financial records leak  
  These pose severe regulatory, financial, and reputational damage.

- **Moderate risk (6):**
  - Business email compromise  
  Common attack vector with high potential impact.

- **Lower risks (4–3):**
  - Theft and supply chain disruption  
  Less frequent or lower overall impact but still require mitigation.

---

## Summary

This assessment demonstrates how risk registers can be used to evaluate and prioritise threats to critical business assets. By calculating likelihood and severity scores, security teams can focus their efforts on the most critical risks, helping to reduce the likelihood of data breaches, financial loss, and operational disruption.
