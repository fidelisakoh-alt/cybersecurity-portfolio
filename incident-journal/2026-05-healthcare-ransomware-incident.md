# Incident Journal: Healthcare Ransomware Incident

## Date

16 May 2026

## Entry

1

## Description

This journal entry documents a ransomware incident affecting a small healthcare clinic. The incident caused major operational disruption because employees were unable to access medical records, files, and systems required to perform their work.

---

## Tools Used

No cybersecurity tools were used in this scenario. The incident was identified through employee reports, ransom notes displayed on affected computers, and investigation of the phishing email and malicious attachment.

---

## The 5 W's

### Who caused the incident?

The incident was caused by an organised group of unethical hackers known to target healthcare and transportation organisations.

### What happened?

Employees received targeted phishing emails containing a malicious attachment. When the attachment was downloaded, malware was installed and ransomware was deployed, encrypting critical company files and preventing access to medical records and business systems.

### When did the incident occur?

The incident occurred on a Tuesday morning at approximately 9:00 a.m.

### Where did the incident happen?

The incident happened at a small U.S. healthcare clinic that provides primary-care services.

### Why did the incident happen?

The incident happened because attackers used phishing emails to trick employees into downloading a malicious attachment. This gave the attackers access to the network and allowed them to deploy ransomware that encrypted critical files.

---

## Additional Notes

This incident shows how phishing can lead to serious business disruption, especially in healthcare where access to patient data is critical. Useful follow-up actions would include employee phishing awareness training, stronger email filtering, endpoint protection, offline backups, and an incident response plan for ransomware events.

## Summary

This scenario demonstrates how a phishing email can lead to ransomware deployment and major disruption to critical services. The incident highlights the importance of prevention, detection, user awareness, secure backups, and a clear ransomware response process.
